# Crawl Space Encapsulation in Raleigh, NC

Your crawl space is the foundation of your home's comfort and durability. If moisture is entering—whether through vents, groundwater, or humidity—it's working against you. Crawl space encapsulation seals and conditions your crawl space, stopping moisture at the source. We've helped hundreds of Raleigh and Wake County homeowners reclaim healthy, stable crawl spaces and the energy savings that follow.

## Why Crawl Space Encapsulation Matters for Raleigh Homes

The Triangle sits in the Piedmont, where red clay soils (Cecil and Appling series) hold moisture and drain slowly. Combined with our humid subtropical climate—summers hitting 70–90% relative humidity and annual rainfall of 43–50 inches—many crawl spaces become moisture traps.

Most homes built in the 1990s through early 2010s were constructed with vented crawl spaces. The theory was that outside air would dry them out. In reality, warm, humid air gets drawn into the crawl space through those vents, condenses on cool surfaces, and never truly dries. That moisture fuels mold, weakens wood structure, feeds pests, and creates the musty smell that rises into living spaces above.

Here's the physics: roughly 40–60% of the air in your home rises from the crawl space through the stack effect. When that air is moist and contaminated, your entire home suffers—higher heating and cooling costs, uncomfortable humidity indoors, and accelerated deterioration of wooden joists and beams. Encapsulation stops this cycle.

## Our Crawl Space Encapsulation Process

We've refined this process over 15 years of Wake County work. Here's what happens:

**1. Inspection & Assessment**
We crawl under your home and document moisture conditions, structural issues, soil type, and existing drainage. We measure humidity levels and check for mold, standing water, and pest entry points. This tells us exactly what we're working with and helps us recommend the right dehumidifier size for your space.

**2. Prep & Cleanup**
We remove debris, old insulation (if vented), and any obvious contaminants. We address standing water or wet spots—either with sump pump installation or improved grading. A clean crawl space is a dry crawl space.

**3. Vapor Barrier Installation**
We install 20-mil polyethylene barrier across the entire floor and extend it 12–18 inches up all perimeter walls. We seal seams with compatible tape. The barrier is the core of encapsulation—it blocks water vapor from the soil and prevents it from rising into your home. This alone can cut crawl space humidity by 30–40%.

**4. Vent & Rim Joist Sealing**
We seal all foundation vents (no more of that humid outdoor air). We caulk and seal the rim joist area where the top of the foundation meets the wooden frame—a common air leak and thermal weak point. We may add foam board or spray foam insulation here for added R-value.

**5. Dehumidifier Installation**
An encapsulated crawl space still needs active humidity control. We install a crawl space dehumidifier sized to your square footage (typically 40–70 pints/day for a 1,200–1,500 sq ft crawl space). The unit runs continuously in warm months and cycles in winter, maintaining indoor conditions at 45–55% relative humidity. This prevents mold regrowth and wood rot.

**6. Final Verification**
We retest humidity levels, ensure all connections are tight, and show you how the system works. You'll know exactly how to operate and maintain your new encapsulated crawl space.

## Signs You Need Crawl Space Encapsulation

- **Musty, damp odors rising into living spaces** – A telltale sign of mold and bacterial growth in the crawl space
- **High humidity inside your home** – Readings above 60% indoors, especially in summer
- **Visible mold on joists, band board, or insulation** – Black or greenish patches indicate active growth
- **Wood rot, soft spots, or deteriorated wood** – Moisture-damaged structural members need immediate attention
- **Cold floors, especially over the crawl space** – Poor insulation and thermal bridging from unconditioned space below
- **Musty smell in closets or basement areas** – That air is coming from below and circulating through your home
- **Condensation on HVAC ducts or pipes** – Moisture-laden air in the crawl space condenses on cooler surfaces
- **Pest activity or entry holes** – Open vents and cracks invite rodents, insects, and wildlife

If you're checking boxes on this list, encapsulation is the investment that stops problems, not just patches them.

## Crawl Space Encapsulation Cost in Raleigh

Let's be transparent. A complete encapsulation project in Wake County typically runs **$5,000–$15,000**, with most homeowners spending **$5,100–$8,250**.

What affects the price:

- **Crawl space size** – Larger footprints need more barrier material and a bigger dehumidifier
- **Soil conditions** – Standing water, heavy clay, or poor drainage means extra prep and possibly sump pump installation
- **Existing damage** – Rotten rim joists or structural repair adds labor and materials
- **Vent & sealing complexity** – Foundation geometry, multiple entry points, and rim joist width all factor in
- **Dehumidifier capacity** – A 2,000 sq ft space needs a larger, more expensive unit than a 1,200 sq ft space
- **Add-ons** – Insulation, advanced vapor barriers, or radon mitigation increase the scope

We provide a detailed quote after inspection—no surprises. And encapsulation is an investment that pays back through energy savings (10–20% reduction in heating/cooling costs), avoided water damage repairs, and a home you can actually enjoy without that basement-y smell.

**Do I need a dehumidifier if my crawl space is already encapsulated?**
Yes. Even an encapsulated crawl space will accumulate humidity from air leaks, water intrusion, and natural moisture migration. A dehumidifier maintains 45–55% relative humidity, the sweet spot that prevents mold while protecting wood. We recommend a crawl space-specific dehumidifier, sized to your square footage. Learn more on our [encapsulated crawl space dehumidifier page](/encapsulated-crawl-space-dehumidifier-raleigh-nc).

**How long does crawl space encapsulation last?**
A properly installed encapsulation system lasts 20–30 years or longer. The 20-mil polyethylene barrier degrades slowly and remains effective for decades. Seals, caulking, and tape may need touch-ups every 10–15 years, depending on foundation movement and settling. Regular dehumidifier maintenance—filter changes, drain cleaning—keeps the system running efficiently. We're happy to inspect your encapsulation during routine maintenance visits.

**What size dehumidifier do I need for a 1,500 sq ft crawl space?**
A 1,500 sq ft crawl space typically needs a 50–70 pint-per-day dehumidifier. Exact size depends on baseline humidity, ventilation, and whether you're in an actively wet area. Our inspection determines the right capacity for your space. Oversizing won't hurt—it just uses more electricity. Undersizing leaves you with mold risk. Let us help you size it correctly; call (919) 867-0580 for a free assessment.
